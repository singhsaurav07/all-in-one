Negative marking in Tier 2 Paper 1 is a full −1 mark per wrong answer — noticeably stricter than Tier 1. Every section of Paper 1 must be individually cleared; a strong Maths score cannot offset a failed English section.

Why this matters for planning

Tier 1 treats all four subjects as equals — 25 marks each. Tier 2 does not: English & Comprehension alone carries 135 of the 390 scored marks in Paper 1, more than Maths and Reasoning combined with GA. A combined study plan should build breadth for Tier 1 first, then tilt hard toward English depth and advanced Maths for Tier 2.

2.1Tier 1 Syllabus — Subject by Subject

Quantitative Aptitude

  • Number system, simplification, HCF/LCM steady
  • Percentage, ratio & proportion high
  • Profit, loss & discount high
  • Simple & compound interest steady
  • Time, speed & distance; time & work steady
  • Average, mixture & alligation
  • Mensuration (2D & 3D)
  • Geometry — lines, triangles, circles high
  • Algebra — basic identities, equations high
  • Trigonometry & heights/distances
  • Data interpretation — tables, bar & pie charts

General Intelligence & Reasoning

  • Analogy & classification high
  • Series — number, alphabet, figure high
  • Coding–decoding
  • Blood relations & direction sense
  • Syllogism & statement–conclusion steady
  • Venn diagrams & figure counting
  • Non-verbal: mirror & water images, paper folding
  • Matrix & word formation
  • Sitting arrangement & puzzles steady

English Comprehension

  • Error detection & sentence improvement high
  • Fill in the blanks & cloze passage high
  • Synonyms, antonyms & homonyms
  • One-word substitution
  • Idioms & phrases
  • Spelling / misspelt word correction
  • Active–passive voice; direct–indirect speech
  • Sentence rearrangement (jumbled words/sentences)
  • Reading comprehension high

General Awareness

  • Current affairs — last 6–12 months high
  • Static GK: books, awards, important days
  • History — freedom movement, ancient/medieval highlights
  • Geography — India-focused, physical & economic
  • Polity — Constitution, governance bodies
  • Economy — basic concepts, budget, schemes
  • General science — physics, chemistry, biology basics steady

3.1Paper 1 — Compulsory for Every Post

Mathematical Abilities

  • Same base topics as Tier 1 Quant, at a deeper, more calculation-heavy level
  • Advanced algebra & polynomial identities high
  • Geometry & coordinate geometry high
  • Trigonometry incl. heights & distances steady
  • Mensuration — 3D solids in more depth
  • Data interpretation — multi-step, comparative
  • Statistics & probability basics rising

Reasoning & General Intelligence

  • All Tier 1 reasoning topics, longer and more layered puzzles
  • Verbal reasoning: syllogism, statement-conclusion steady
  • Non-verbal reasoning: series, mirror/water images
  • Higher-order puzzles & seating arrangement high
  • Data sufficiency & logical Venn diagrams

English Language & Comprehension

  • Grammar & vocabulary — the largest single weight block in Tier 2 highest
  • Cloze test & para-jumbles
  • Reading comprehension — longer passages, more of them high
  • Error spotting & sentence improvement
  • One-word substitution, idioms, phrasal verbs

General Awareness

  • Same domains as Tier 1, with more static/factual depth
  • Current affairs, 8–10 months prior to exam steady
  • Schemes, appointments, awards, sports
  • Economic & scientific developments in the news

Section III — Qualifying Modules

Computer Knowledge Test

  • Computer basics, hardware & software
  • MS Office (Word, Excel, PowerPoint)
  • Internet, email, networking basics
  • Basic cyber security & abbreviations

Qualifying only — must clear, doesn't add to merit marks.

Data Entry Speed Test (DEST)

  • Typing a given passage on computer within a fixed time
  • Assessed on speed, not on multiple-choice marks

Qualifying only. Practise typing speed weekly — this is a habit, not a topic.

3.2Paper 2 & Paper 3 — Post-Specific

Paper 2 — Statistics

  • Collection, classification & presentation of data
  • Measures of central tendency & dispersion
  • Correlation & regression
  • Probability theory & random variables
  • Sampling theory & statistical inference
  • Analysis of variance, time series
  • Index numbers

Paper 3 — Finance, Accounts & Economics

  • Basic accounting principles & financial statements
  • Fundamentals of auditing
  • Economic concepts — demand, supply, markets
  • Indian economy & government finance
  • Role of government: budgeting, fiscal policy

Only attempt Paper 2 or Paper 3 if you're targeting JSO or AAO posts specifically. For most other posts, mastering Paper 1 is the entire Tier 2 job.
